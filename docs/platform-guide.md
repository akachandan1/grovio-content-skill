# Platform Content Guide

> Platform-specific rules, psychology, and formatting standards used by the Grovio Content Skill.

Each platform has its own native psychology — what works on LinkedIn often fails on Twitter, and vice versa. This guide explains the rules the skill applies per platform.

---

## LinkedIn

### Platform Psychology
LinkedIn activates **professional identity** and **career status** psychology. Users share content that makes them look informed, forward-thinking, and credible to their professional network. The dominant triggers are **Authority**, **Identity Signaling**, and **Curiosity Gap**.

### Hook Rules
- **First line is everything.** Only the first 1-2 lines show before "see more." This is the only real estate that matters.
- **Never start with:** "I'm excited to share...", "Happy to announce...", "Proud to...", "It's been a journey..."
- **Start with:** A bold claim, a surprising data point, a contrarian statement, or a specific question
- **The test:** Cover the rest of the post. Would you still click "see more" from the hook alone?

### Format Options

| Format | Best For | Character Range |
|---|---|---|
| Short punchy post | Opinion, hot take, single insight | 600-1000 chars |
| Long-form story | Personal narrative, transformation, lessons | 1000-1800 chars |
| Numbered list | Data-driven insights, frameworks, tips | 800-1400 chars |
| Contrarian take | Thought leadership, debate-starter | 700-1200 chars |
| Single-stat drop | Surprising data + implications | 400-700 chars |

### Structure Template
```
[Hook — 1-2 lines, bold claim or curiosity gap]

[Context — why this matters, 2-3 lines]

[Core insight or story — the substance]

[Takeaway — specific, actionable, memorable]

[CTA — ONE of: question OR soft CTA. Never both. Never neither.]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3 (3-5 max, niche over broad)
```

### Engagement Drivers (ranked)
1. Contrarian opinions backed by data
2. Personal stories with transferable lessons
3. Data that surprises (stats nobody knows)
4. Questions that spark genuine debate
5. Behind-the-scenes / honest admissions

### What Kills LinkedIn Posts
- Starting with "I" as the very first word (weak hook)
- More than 5 hashtags (looks spammy)
- Multiple CTAs (creates decision paralysis)
- Emojis used as bullet points excessively
- Vague takeaways ("remember to be authentic")
- Posting without a clear point of view

### Voice Calibration
LinkedIn voice should be: **authoritative but human**. First-person experience is the highest trust signal on LinkedIn. Telling what you've lived through > telling what others should do.

## Twitter / X

### Platform Psychology
Twitter activates **real-time discourse** and **in-group identity** psychology. The dominant behavior is hot takes, wit, and tribal signaling. Shares happen when content makes the sharer look smart, informed, or part of a movement. Dominant triggers: **Curiosity Gap**, **Surprise**, **Identity Signaling**.

### Single Tweet Rules
- **240 characters max.** The hook IS the entire post.
- Must be retweetable as a standalone sentence.
- Should work without any context.
- **The screenshot test:** Would someone screenshot just this tweet?

### Thread Rules
- **Tweet 1 (Hook):** Must earn the click on its own. Best hook types:
  - The Prediction: "X will replace Y by 2027"
  - The Contradiction: "Everyone says X. The data shows Y."
  - The Number: "7 things top founders never tell you"
  - The Confession: "I was completely wrong about X"
  - The Provocation: "Hot take: X is actually bad for your career"
- **Tweets 2-N:** Each tweet = one complete idea. No cliffhangers mid-tweet.
- **Last tweet:** Summary of the key insight + soft CTA (follow, share, reply)
- **Optimal length:** 5-8 tweets. Drops off sharply after 10.

### Viral Mechanics
- **Quotable lines** — single sentences that stand alone as wisdom
- **Specific numbers** — "3x", "47%", "$1M" outperform vague claims
- **Tribe signals** — "if you're a founder / builder / designer, you know this"
- **Format contrast** — short single tweet among threads, or vice versa

### Hook Type Reference

| Hook Type | Template | Example |
|---|---|---|
| The Prediction | "[X] will [outcome] by [date]" | "Remote work will outlast the office by 2030." |
| The Contradiction | "Everyone says [X]. But [Y]." | "Everyone says ship fast. But quality compounds." |
| The Number | "[N] [things] [audience] [outcome]" | "5 things every SaaS founder learns too late." |
| The Confession | "I was wrong about [X]." | "I was wrong about paid ads for 3 years." |
| The Data | "[Surprising stat]. Here's why it matters." | "95% of content gets zero shares. Here's the 5% pattern." |
| The Provocation | "Hot take: [contrarian belief]" | "Hot take: your landing page doesn't need a hero image." |

## Instagram

### Platform Psychology
Instagram activates **aspiration**, **aesthetics**, and **lifestyle** psychology. Content is primarily visual — the caption is secondary but still critical. The dominant behavior is saving (bookmarking) and sharing to stories. Dominant triggers: **Belonging**, **Awe**, **Identity Signaling**.

### Caption Rules
- **First line = the most valuable real estate.** Only this shows in the feed before "more."
- Must be compelling enough to make the user tap to expand.
- After the first line: Story or value delivery, then insight, then CTA.

### Caption Length Guide

| Type | Length | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Short punch | 100-150 chars | Bold statement, quote, single insight |
| Standard | 200-350 chars | Brand story, product feature |
| Storytelling | 350-600 chars | Personal narrative, deep insight |
| Educational | 600-1000 chars | Tutorial, tips, how-to |

### Hashtag Strategy
- **5-10 hashtags** (not 30 — that era is over)
- Mix ratio: 40% niche (10K-200K posts), 40% mid (200K-2M), 20% broad (2M+)
- Place at end of caption or in first comment
- Use hashtags your specific audience follows — not just high-volume generic ones

### CTA Options (ranked by effectiveness)
1. "Save this for later" — drives saves = algorithmic boost
2. "Share with someone who needs this" — drives shares
3. "Comment [word/number] if this is you" — drives comments
4. "What would you add?" — open question
5. "Link in bio" — lowest engagement, use sparingly

### Emotional Tone
Instagram captions should feel more **personal and visual** than LinkedIn. Describe what should be *seen* or *felt*. Use present tense. Write as if talking to one person.

## Email

### Platform Psychology
Email is a **personal channel** — the reader has already opted in. The dominant psychology is **trusted advisor relationship**. There is no social sharing motivation — success here is opens, clicks, and replies. Dominant triggers: **Reciprocity**, **Personalization**, **Curiosity Gap**, **Loss Aversion**.

### Subject Line Rules
- **6-8 words ideal.** The entire persuasion job happens here.
- Embed the primary psychological trigger in the subject line.
- **Always provide an A/B variant** — one curiosity-gap, one benefit-forward
- **Never:** "Newsletter #47", "Our latest update", "Hi [First Name]" (as full subject)

### Subject Line Formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| The Curiosity Gap | "The mistake 80% of founders make" |
| The Direct Benefit | "Triple your LinkedIn reach this week" |
| The Data Hook | "Why 95% of content gets zero shares" |
| The Contrarian | "Stop optimizing your landing page" |
| The Personalized | "[First Name], your competitors are doing this" |
| The Urgency | "This closes tomorrow" |

### Preview Text
- Completes or extends the subject line — never repeats it
- Should deepen the curiosity gap opened by the subject
- 40-100 characters

### Email Structure

```
Subject:  [Primary trigger in 6-8 words]
Preview:  [Extends subject line curiosity — don't repeat it]

Opening:  [Must match energy of subject — no let-down. 1-2 lines.]

Body:
  → Hook / problem (2-3 sentences)
  → Value delivery (the core content)
  → Bridge to CTA

CTA:      [ONE action. Specific verb. Specific benefit.]
          "Read the full breakdown →"
          "Claim your free audit →"
          "Join 2,000 founders →"
```

### Length Guide

| Email Type | Length | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement / trigger | 100-200 words | Drive a single click |
| Nurture | 300-500 words | Build relationship + credibility |
| Educational | 500-800 words | Deliver real value, position as expert |
| Newsletter | 600-1200 words | Multiple value items + links |

### Rules
- **One CTA per email.** Multiple CTAs split attention and reduce conversion.
- **Never start with "I"** — start with "you" or the reader's situation
- **P.S. line** — one of the most-read parts of any email. Use it for the second most important message.

## Blog / Long-form

### Platform Psychology
Blog content is consumed by **high-ELM, research-mode** audiences. They are evaluating you. Trust is built through depth, specificity, and evidence. The dominant motivation is finding an answer or building knowledge. Dominant triggers: **Authority**, **Reciprocity**, **Utility**.

### Title Formula
```
[Primary Keyword] + [Power Word] + [Specific Benefit or Number]
```

Examples:
- "AI Marketing for Startups: The 2026 Playbook"
- "7 LinkedIn Strategies That Actually Drive Pipeline"
- "Why Your Content Isn't Working (And the Fix Is Simpler Than You Think)"

### Meta Description
- 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword naturally
- End with a curiosity gap or clear benefit promise

### Post Structure

```
Title (H1)        → Keyword + power word + promise
Meta description  → 150-160 chars with keyword + hook

Introduction (150-200 words)
  1. Hook — stat, question, or bold statement
  2. Problem agitation — describe the reader's pain
  3. Promise — what they'll get from this post
  4. Bridge — transition into content

Body (H2/H3 sections)
  → Each H2 = one complete valuable idea
  → Each section includes: data point + example + actionable tip
  → Bold key insights and takeaways
  → Use numbered lists and bullets for scannability
  → Target: 5-8 H2 sections

Brand/Product callout (1-2 per post)
  → Natural, non-pushy placement
  → "How [Brand] handles this: [1-2 sentences]"

Conclusion (100-150 words)
  → 3 key takeaways
  → Reinforce problem + solution
  → Strong CTA

FAQ Section (optional — great for SEO featured snippets)
  → 4-6 questions as H3
  → 40-60 word answers
```

### SEO Checklist
- [ ] Primary keyword in: title, first paragraph, 1-2 H2s, meta description
- [ ] Secondary keywords (3-5) distributed naturally
- [ ] Internal links to related content
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (data claims)
- [ ] Target word count: 1,500-2,500 standard; 3,000+ for pillar content
- [ ] Table of contents for posts over 1,500 words
- [ ] Flesch readability score: 60+ (smart but conversational)

### Writing Standards
- **Specific > vague:** "lift trial-to-paid from 5% to 12%" not "increase conversions"
- **Examples over claims:** show, don't just tell
- **Short paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences max for online reading
- **Conversational intelligence:** smart founder talking to another smart founder

## Cross-Platform Adaptation Rules

When adapting one core piece of content across platforms:

**Never** just resize the same post. Each platform requires genuine native adaptation.

| Adaptation | What Changes | What Stays |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn → Twitter | Remove context (Twitter readers have none), make every line standalone, add thread structure | Core insight, data points |
| LinkedIn → Instagram | Visual-first framing, shorter paragraphs, warmer personal tone | Core message, emotional hook |
| Blog → LinkedIn | Compress to 1-2 key insights, add opinion/POV, remove academic tone | Core data, main argument |
| Blog → Email | Direct personal address, single CTA, urgency/relevance framing | Core value, key data |
| Email → Social | Remove personalization tokens, make context-independent, add identity signal | Core hook, primary trigger |

**The 10-word test:** Summarize the core insight in 10 words. If every adaptation doesn't deliver that 10-word idea — you've drifted.
