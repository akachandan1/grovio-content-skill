## The 25 Psychological Triggers

### Social Triggers
| # | Trigger | Definition | Content Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Social Proof | People follow what others do | Testimonials, user counts, "X companies use this" |
| 2 | Authority | People defer to credible experts | Credentials, data citations, expert endorsements |
| 3 | Liking | People say yes to people they like | Founder voice, shared values, warmth |
| 4 | Unity | People respond to shared identity ("we") | "For founders like us", community framing |
| 5 | Bandwagon | People join what's already popular | Momentum language, growth numbers |

### Scarcity & Urgency Triggers
| # | Trigger | Definition | Content Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | Scarcity | Limited supply = higher perceived value | "Only 50 spots", "Limited beta access" |
| 7 | Urgency | Deadline = action now | "Closes Friday", "Offer ends soon" |
| 8 | FOMO | Fear of missing out on what others have | "Others are already doing X" |
| 9 | Exclusivity | Belonging to a chosen group | "Invite-only", "For serious founders only" |

### Cognitive Bias Triggers
| # | Trigger | Definition | Content Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | Loss Aversion | Fear of losing > desire for gaining | "Stop losing leads", "You're leaving money on the table" |
| 11 | Anchoring | First number heard sets the reference | Show high value before price |
| 12 | Framing Effect | Same fact, different frame = different response | "90% success rate" vs "10% failure rate" |
| 13 | Availability Bias | Recent/vivid = more likely | Specific recent stories outperform general claims |
| 14 | Confirmation Bias | People seek information confirming beliefs | Lead with their existing belief, then reframe |
| 15 | Cognitive Fluency | Easy to process = more trustworthy | Short sentences, clear structure, simple words |

### Emotional Triggers
| # | Trigger | Definition | Content Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| 16 | Curiosity Gap | Incomplete information compels closure | "Here's what nobody tells you about..." |
| 17 | Surprise | Unexpected breaks pattern and demands attention | Counterintuitive stats, unexpected confessions |
| 18 | Awe | Scale, beauty, or transcendence creates sharing | Category-defining claims, paradigm shifts |
| 19 | Fear | Threat of pain motivates action | Risk-of-inaction scenarios, stakes-setting |
| 20 | Belonging | Need to be part of something larger | Community, movement, tribe language |

### Motivation Triggers
| # | Trigger | Definition | Content Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| 21 | Reciprocity | Give first; they feel obligated to return | Free frameworks, genuine value before ask |
| 22 | Commitment & Consistency | People honor past commitments | "You already believe X — so why not Y?" |
| 23 | Progress Effect | Visible progress increases motivation | Milestones, streaks, "you're almost there" |
| 24 | Personalization | Content that feels "for me" converts better | Role-specific hooks, segment-aware language |
| 25 | Identity Signaling | Sharing content = signal about who I am | Make sharing say something about the sharer |

## Three-Brain Rule

Every piece of content must activate all three brain systems. Miss one = missed conversion.

| Brain | System | Needs | Content Layer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emotional | Limbic | Feeling, story, identity | Narrative + emotional trigger + first-person stakes |
| Social | Prefrontal | Belonging, proof, norms | Tribe signal + social proof + "people like us" |
| Rational | Neocortex | Logic, value, evidence | Data + utility + specific benefit |

**Test:** Hit 1 brain = ignored. Hit 2 = engaged. Hit all 3 = shared.

## Psychological Scoring Rubric (60 Points)

Score each content variant across 6 dimensions, 0–10 each:

| Dimension | 0–3 | 4–6 | 7–9 | 10 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Trigger Potency** | No clear trigger | Weak trigger present | Strong primary trigger | Multiple stacked triggers, synergistic |
| **Emotional Resonance** | No emotional response | Mild engagement | Strong emotional pull | Reader feels it personally |
| **Brand Voice Fidelity** | Sounds generic | Partially on-brand | Clearly this brand | Unmistakably them without the logo |
| **Originality** | Clichéd, heard before | Slightly fresh angle | Distinctive perspective | First-mover angle, own-able |
| **Utility** | No practical value | Some value | Clear actionable insight | Changes how reader thinks or acts |
| **Virality Potential** | No share motivation | Might share | Likely to share | Screenshot-worthy, share-reflex |

**Composite /60:**
- 50–60: Publish immediately
- 40–49: Publish with minor optimization
- 30–39: A/B test — strong but not ready
- Below 30: Rewrite before publishing

## NeuroScore Rubric (50 Points)

Source: TRIBE v2, Meta FAIR (d'Ascoli et al., arXiv:2507.22229, 2026) — fMRI brain encoding model trained on 700+ volunteers. Key finding: multimodal content significantly outperforms unimodal in high-level associative cortices (TPJ, MTG, DMN). Framework operationalises these findings as scorable dimensions.

Score each variant across 5 neurological dimensions, 0–10 each:

| Dimension | What It Measures | 0–3 | 7–10 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cortical Coverage** | Engagement across distinct brain regions | Single modality (text only, no imagery, no story) | Activates visual, auditory, language, and emotional cortices simultaneously |
| **Multimodal Synergy** | How effectively modalities reinforce each other | Modalities contradict or ignore each other | Audio, visual, text all reinforce the same message |
| **Emotional-Rational Calibration** | Emotional/rational balance matches funnel stage | Wrong balance for stage (emotional at conversion, rational at awareness) | TOF: 80% emotional; BOF: 70% rational |
| **Linguistic Complexity** | Hook/CTA simplicity vs body depth | Uniform complexity throughout (all simple OR all complex) | Simple hook + CTA; complex body with causal connectors |
| **Attention Integration** | Pattern interrupts, tension, spatial language | No pattern interrupt, flat narrative | Opens with pattern break, sustains tension, uses spatial cues |

**NeuroScore /50:**
- 40–50: Neurologically optimized — maximum encoding
- 30–39: Strong — minor adjustments improve retention
- 20–29: Moderate — rewire emotional-rational balance
- Below 20: Fundamental structural rewrite needed

## Combined 110-Point Scoring System

Every content-generating skill uses this combined system:

| Combined Score (/110) | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| 90–110 | **Publish immediately** — highest confidence |
| 75–89 | **A/B test** — strong, validate with real data |
| 60–74 | **Conditional** — optimize hook or trigger first |
| Below 60 | **Mandatory rewrite** — do not publish |

**Always report both scores:** `Psychological: [n]/60 + NeuroScore: [n]/50 = [n]/110`

## Universal Quality Gates

Run this checklist on every variant before delivering:

- [ ] **3-Brain Test** — Emotional (limbic) + Social (prefrontal) + Rational (neocortex) all activated?
- [ ] **Hook Test** — Cover the rest. Does the first line earn attention standalone?
- [ ] **Identity Signal** — "Sharing this = I am ___" — completable with something the audience wants to be?
- [ ] **Fogg Test** — Motivation present? Ability present (easy to act)? Trigger present (clear CTA)?
- [ ] **Brand Fidelity** — Remove the brand name. Still sounds exactly like them?
- [ ] **Format Test** — Native to the channel? Not just resized from another platform?
- [ ] **110-Point Gate** — Score ≥75/110 before delivering. Flag anything below 60 as mandatory rewrite.

## Voice Standards

### Brand Voice
- Speaks as the company: "We", "Our team", "[Company]"
- Trigger stack: Social Proof (#1), Authority (#2), Bandwagon (#5), Unity (#4)
- Tone: More structured, data-forward, professional
- Reach: Lower organic on LinkedIn/Twitter; higher enterprise trust
- Best for: Product launches, company milestones, case studies, hiring

### Founder Voice
- Speaks as a person: "I", first-person experience, honest admissions
- Trigger stack: Liking (#3), Reciprocity (#21), Identity Signaling (#25), Belonging (#20)
- Tone: Conversational, story-driven, opinion-heavy
- Reach: 3–5x more organic reach on LinkedIn/Twitter vs brand accounts
- Best for: Thought leadership, building-in-public, contrarian takes, community
- **Rule:** If brand has a founder with a public profile, default to founder voice for thought-leadership content

**Auto-detect:** Goal contains "thought-leadership", "opinion", "building-in-public", "story" → default to founder voice.
